About

I am a Graduate Researcher at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln, focusing on the intersection of materials science and electrical characterization. My current work involves developing biological field-effect transistors (FETs) for non-invasive diagnostic applications.

At UNL, I develop high-gain biological field-effect transistors for non-invasive microbial electrophysiology — tackling antibiotic resistance with molecular precision.

With over three years of experience in pharmaceutical manufacturing at Eskayef Pharmaceuticals, I bring a unique perspective on reliability, regulatory compliance (FDA/EU-GMP), and system optimization to frontier research. I led reliability-driven improvements that increased production throughput by 30% while maintaining FDA and EU-GMP compliance.
